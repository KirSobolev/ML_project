ML Models
XGBoost
XGBoost, short for eXtreme Gradient Boosting, is a powerful machine learning algorithm known for its efficiency, speed, and accuracy. It belongs to the family of boosting algorithms, which are ensemble learning techniques that combine the predictions of multiple weak learners, usually decision trees, to c reate a strong predictive model. XGBoost is particularly effective for handling large datasets and complex data patterns, making it a popular choice for various machine learning tasks . . .
CNNs
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are a type of deep learning algorithm specifically designed for processing structured grid data like images. They use layers of convolutional filters to automatically and adaptively learn spatial hierarchies of features from input images, making them highly effective for tasks such as image recognition, classification, and segmentation . . .
Autoregressive (AR)
An Autoregressive (AR) model is a type of statistical model used for time series forecasting. It predicts future values based on a linear combination of past values in the series. Essentially, it assumes that past values have a direct influence on future values, making it useful for analyzing and forecasting time-dependent data . . .
Meta's Prophet
Prophet is an open-source forecasting tool developed by Meta (formerly Facebook). It's designed for time series forecasting and is particularly effective for data with strong seasonal effects and multiple seasonality. Prophet decomposes time series data into trend, seasonality, and holidays, making it easy to understand and interpret the results. It's user-friendly and requires minimal data preprocessing, making it accessible for analysts and data scientists . . .
Neural Prophet
Neural Prophet is a time series forecasting framework that combines the strengths of traditional time series models and deep learning methods. Built on PyTorch, Neural Prophet is inspired by Facebook's Prophet and AR-Net. It offers a modular architecture, allowing users to customize and extend the model easily. Neural Prophet is designed for high-frequency and long-duration time series data, providing interpretable and scalable forecasting solutions . . .

With the help of CNN we were able to make friction classification predictions. We assigned friction classes based on friction, 0 - worst possible and 3 - great friction. Models’ overall accuracy is around 90%. However, there is a problem: class 0 and 1 (in which we are interested the most) are highly underrepresented and as a result the model is biased towards classes 2 and 3. That means that model is good in recognizing 2 and 3, but it’s hard to predict 0 and 1.
